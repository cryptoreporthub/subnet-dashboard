How does this work?
High Volatility — Breakout/breakdown risk
These subnets see large price swings (top 25% by volatility). They may present short-term trading opportunities, but carry higher risk. Good for momentum plays if you have conviction.
Core Volatility — Trend-following zone
Moderate, average volatility (middle 50%). Steady price action — enough movement to profit from trends without getting whipped around. Healthiest zone for momentum strategies.
Low Volatility — Consolidation
Tight price range, barely moving (bottom 25%). A breakout may be imminent — these are "coiled springs." Lower risk but less immediate opportunity. Watch for volume spikes signaling an impending move.
📋 How the score is calculated
Each subnet's volatility score = max(|24h price change|, |7d price change| ÷ 7)
Subnets are then split into quartiles: the top 25% are "High", the bottom 25% are "Low", and the middle 50% are "Core".
The dashed line on each bar shows where the mean volatility sits — bars extending past it are above average.
